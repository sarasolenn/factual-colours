FACTUAL COLOURS


How can graphic design make complex information accessible? Factual Colours provides introductions to research through colour palettes. This example presents research on the human impact on the Earth's water cycle.

There is a gap in how information and data are conveyed today. Many are interested in new research but the findings are not sufficiently accessible for those who do not have advanced knowledge in the field. Therefore, I wanted to explore new methods to simplify and visualise information that makes it easier to read and to comprehend. The purpose of this project is to create reflection on how graphic design can make something more tangible and how colour can be a way to convey knowledge in a clear way.



by Sara Solén 2022

INTRODUCTION

Among the first things we are taught at school is the water cycle. It makes water seem like Earth’s infinite resource. What we likely did not get to learn is how humanity has affected and keeps affecting the cycle resulting in consequences for all life on Earth.

This is a colour scheme which represents the different stages of water in the Earth’s water cycle and gives an easily understood introduction to Stockholm Resilience Center’s latest research on the topic.
